## France — England · 18.07, 00:00 MSK · Hard Rock Stadium, Miami

**The third-place play-off — "the consolation final nobody wanted." Deschamps takes charge for the last time after 14 years; Mbappé is one goal from the Golden Boot and plays a day before Messi's final. History screams "goals" (no third-place match in World Cup history has ever finished goalless), but England-2018 is a reminder that a top favourite's rotation can also produce a dull 2:0.**

## Key points

- **Deschamps — his last match, officially.** He leaves after 14 years and a record 26 World Cup matches; Zidane is the successor. He himself called the third-place game "completely pointless," but Dembélé promised to give the coach "a memorable send-off." France have a motivational hook despite the "dead rubber" label.
- ⚠️ **Mbappé is one goal from the Boot — the match's main goal driver.** 8 goals (+3 assists) against Messi's 8 (+4); the tie-break is assists, then fewer minutes. A Mbappé goal (his 9th) would force Messi to score in the final; and both Mbappé and Kane shoot a DAY before Messi. Mbappé carries a minor ankle knock from the quarters but was fit by the semi. Kane (6) publicly plays down the race — "the point is to win the World Cup" — so his personal incentive is weaker.
- ⚠️ **Line-ups uncertain on both sides.** For France, Saliba went off on 29 in the semi (back), status unclear; a "mix of reserves and part of the first choice" is expected (not a B-team — tradition plus Deschamps' send-off). For England, Konsa is back from suspension (available for this match), Stones is a doubt after a head clash with Pickford, and Rice is the No. 1 candidate to rest. No official rotation statements yet (pressers on the 17th).
- **Third-place matches are the tournament's most goal-heavy.** Across all World Cup history (20 matches): 0 goalless, average total 3.4–3.8. Over the last 5 World Cups: Over 2.5 — 80%, BTTS — 60%. Few cards (2–5 yellows, 0 red cards across the last 5) — they play relaxed.
- ⚠️ **The counter-example is England-2018 itself.** 0:2 to Belgium, the only low-scoring third-place match in 40 years: the side that lost the semi painfully rotates 5-6 positions, while the opponent fields a near-first-choice XI and cruises. If France field a strong side and England shuffle after the comeback drama with Argentina, a "favourite wins comfortably 2:0" scenario is more likely than a shoot-out.
